The 2024 Hungarian Grand Prix (officially known as the Formula 1 Hungarian Grand Prix 2024) is a Formula One motor race set to take place on 21 July 2024 at the Hungaroring in Mogyoród, Hungary. It will be the thirteenth round of the 2024 Formula One World Championship.


Background[edit]
The event will be held at the Hungaroring in Mogyoród for the 39th time in the circuit's history, across the weekend of 19–21 July.[1] The Grand Prix will be the thirteenth round of the 2024 Formula One World Championship and the 39th running of the Hungarian Grand Prix as a round of the Formula One World Championship.[2]

Championship standings before the race[edit]
Going into the weekend, Max Verstappen leads the Drivers' Championship with 255 points, 84 points ahead of Lando Norris in second, and 105 ahead of Charles Leclerc in third. Red Bull Racing, with 373 points, leads the Constructors' Championship from Ferrari and McLaren, who were second and third with 302 and 295 points, respectively.[3]

Entrants[edit]
See also: 2024 Formula One World Championship § Entries
The drivers and teams are set to be the same as the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for the race.[citation needed] Oliver Bearman is set to drive in the first free practice session for Haas, in place of Kevin Magnussen.[4]

Tyre choices[edit]
Further information: Formula One tyres
Tyre supplier Pirelli will bring the C3, C4, and C5 tyre compounds (the three softest in their range) designated hard, medium, and soft, respectively, for teams to use at this event.[5]

Practice[edit]
Three free practice sessions will be held for the event. The first free practice session will be held on 19 July 2024, at 13:30 local time (UTC+2). The second free practice session will be held on the same day, at 17:00 local time. The third free practice session will be held on 20 July 2024, at 12:30 local time.[6]

Qualifying[edit]
Qualifying will be held on 20 July 2024, at 16:00 local time (UTC+2).[6]

Race[edit]
The race will be held on 21 July 2024, at 15:00 local time (UTC+2), and is set to be run for 70 laps.[6]
